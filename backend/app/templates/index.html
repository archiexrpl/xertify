Frequently asked questions

What is XERTIFY?

XERTIFY is a verifiable truth engine. We take your real-world facts (events, updates, sensor readings, signatures), evaluate them under a published policy at a point in time, and generate cryptographic proof bundles that anyone can verify.

What problem does this solve?

Today, truth lives in PDFs, dashboards, emails, APIs, and databases — and every system asks “Do I trust this source?” XERTIFY flips the model: partners don’t trust us. They verify hashes, schemas, and policy identifiers.

How does verification work?

A verifier fetches a proof bundle and checks: the bundle hash, the verdict_inputs hash, schema validity (object + events), and that the policy hash matches the published policy pack for the vertical. This can be done offline, without trusting our UI.

What do you ask from a company?

One question: “What does truth mean in your industry?” We extract: what objects matter, what events change validity, who is allowed to assert those events, and under what rules something becomes invalid — then we encode it as a verifiable spec.

What do you actually deliver?

You get: a public, machine-readable spec of your truth, proof bundles for every assertion, offline verification for partners and regulators, and automation hooks so your systems can gate actions on verifiable truth.

Is this on the XRPL?

XERTIFY is built to issue and verify XRPL-based assets and verification links. Proof bundles are verifiable offline today. XRPL is the settlement layer for ownership/issuance, and optional anchoring can be enabled when a partner requires public timestamping.

Why is this safe for enterprises?

You’re not locked into us. Proofs are portable. Policies are identified by hash. Schemas are public. If XERTIFY disappeared tomorrow, your proofs would still be verifiable and valid.
